Frequently Asked Questions

What type of power outlets does Taniti use?

Power outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the United States. (Source: About the Island.docx)

Are there restrictions on alcohol sales?

Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m. (Source: About the Island.docx)

What is the drinking age on Taniti?

The drinking age on Taniti is 18. This is not strictly enforced. (Source: About the Island.docx)

Is English widely spoken on the island?

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents. (Source: About the Island.docx)

What medical facilities are available?

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees. (Source: About the Island.docx)

Is Taniti a safe island for tourists?

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes, so visitors should remain aware of their surroundings. (Source: About the Island.docx)

Are attractions and restaurants open on holidays?

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on these days. Visitors should plan accordingly. (Source: About the Island.docx)

What currency is used in Taniti?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency. Many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards. (Source: About the Island.docx)

How do most visitors arrive in Taniti?

Almost all visitors arrive in Taniti by air, though some arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week. (Source: About the Island.docx)

What are the ground transportation options in Taniti?

Public buses serve Taniti City (5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily), and private buses serve the rest of the island. Taxis are available in Taniti City. Rental cars can be rented from a local agency near the airport. Bikes and helmets (helmets are required by law) are available to rent from several vendors. Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable. (Source: About the Island.docx)
